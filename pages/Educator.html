My Past Career as an Educator

Lessons Learned Teaching in Public Schools

  • I am best suited to work with smaller groups of people. I can handle lots of people, but as an introvert, I will find myself very drained after a lot of days with lots of people…
  • You can’t keep moving forward at a decent pace if you're stuck trying to get everything perfect.
  • Appreciate where you’re at in life right now! I’m glad to have had my experience as a teacher, but I’m also glad to have learned enough about myself to work towards a position better suited for my strengths and personality.
  • We all learn things differently. What makes sense to you doesn’t always make sense to someone else. When you're a teacher, this means that you must get creative and present information in lots of different, engaging ways to reach people. The other side of that is, as a learner, you need to find out what is most engaging and effective for you.
  • When you teach something to someone, you’ll come to realize just how much you do, or do not, truly understand that subject.
  • From my years commanding and entertaining the classroom. I am now a proficient and confident public speaker.
  • Teaching is a difficult and undervalued profession. Call or email your favorite teachers and thank them for the impact they had on your life. There is a soft spot in my heart for teachers, they work too hard for not enough.

Background Education & Teaching Experience

  • 7th Grade Science teacher at Bailey STEM Magnet Middle School (2013-2014)
  • 9th-12th Grade History/Government/Economics Teacher at Cheatham County Central High School (2015-2016)

My background area of study was history, but getting started in education, I opted to take on different subject areas to get my foot in the door as a full-time employed teacher. I took the Praxis exam to attain certifications to teach all core areas of 5th-8th grade. With more opportunities for employment, I soon found myself teaching 7th grade Science.

In teaching at the middle school level, in a different subject area, I was forced to take on a different perspective, outside my comfort zone, and develop materials that would make sense to a younger audience than I had previously anticipated. This proved to be difficult, but it made me a better and more prepared teacher. I learned classroom management and was able to focus more on teaching than subject materials.

After getting some professional experience on my resume, I was able to get a full-time position teaching in my subject area of Secondary History at Cheatham County Central High School in 2015.
