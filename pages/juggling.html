Why Juggling?

A fair question. For me it was, because it was around and looked fun. I guess it looked like something I thought I could do. My brother got a book that came with a set of 3 beanbag balls. He read it and taught himself. I just watched. Just playing around with him, I picked it up as well, from there we got more elaborate, doing some passes and other patterns. Then around age 13 one year my grandparents gifted me a set of 3 clubs... I messed around with them until I had it down. Once you have the club and ball patterns down, pretty much every other medium is possible.

Today I do it because I'm ADHD, and its a good way to be moving, and get some energy out in a healthy and meditative way. Juggling has been found to help strengthen areas of the brain associated with movement, and reflexes. I find it a helpful because you can always work at some new pattern or movement, and thats a good way to stay motivated to improve, theres always another acheivable goal ahead of you.

But most importantly, I juggle because it's fun, it brings joy to me, and the people around me.

Juggling Mediums

Balls

Classic 3-ball juggling. Theres so many patterns you can learn, one could specialize in 3-ball patterns and still be considered a master.

Rings

Though not my next learned skill after balls, this is a natual progression from 3-balls. Same cascade patterns with minimal difference, most people can pick up rings soon after they're comfortable with balls. Rings are easier, but tricky with wind outside.

Clubs

I got my first set of clubs from as a gift from my grandparents around the age of 13. I was definitely surprised... I didn't ask for them. Hundreds of smashed noses later, I found myself proficient with clubs! Once you've got the club throw pattern down, knives and torches are just a matter of confidence.

Knives

Knives are heavy clubs, juggled with confidence! They are seriously heavy though, watch the toes!

Torches

Unless you wanna get chainsaws, torches are the pinnacle of juggling. Torches are super cool. they light up the yard at night, and make a sweet noise for an excellent show. Torches do require a bit of extra upkeep and care.

Mixed

The hardest flex is a combination of multiple throw mediums. Although not usually a favorite, this is the most difficult skill to master, its never as comfortable as the single medium, but you can sink into it after a bit of practice. The ball through the ring is a classic.
